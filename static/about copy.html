Liturgica Poloniae: A Descriptive Catalogue of Polish Liturgical Manuscripts (Part 1: Codices Copied until ca. 1300).

GOALS OF THE PROJECT:

The project, conducted at the Institute of Art, Polish Academy of Sciences, aims to meticulously catalogue approximately 110 liturgical manuscripts copied until ca. 1300, currently held in Polish and foreign repositories. The absence of a comprehensive publication addressing this objective and the inadequacies of existing catalogues necessitate a detailed examination of Polish liturgical codices.

Utilizing advanced technologies and a multidisciplinary team of experts, the project seeks to provide reliable information about Polish liturgical manuscripts. The resulting English-language catalogue will offer extensive details on bindings, scripts, musical notations, decoration, provenance, dating, and, notably, a specific description of content tailored to liturgical genres. The catalogue will be published in open access and distributed to major libraries.

The online platform, available at this site, utilizes SQL database and AI components for semi-automated analysis of liturgical manuscripts and presents the results of the project in a way that enables multi-faceted searches with divergent queries.

The project intends to facilitate global research on Poland’s national heritage, correct misdated or misidentified manuscripts, and aid various scholars and the broader public in interpreting liturgical sources.
